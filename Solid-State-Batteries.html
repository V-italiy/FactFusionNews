Unlike conventional lithium-ion batteries, which use liquid electrolytes, solid-state batteries employ a solid electrolyte. This design offers several advantages: higher energy density, reduced risk of overheating, and faster charging capabilities. Solid-state batteries eliminate this risk, thanks to their non-flammable electrolyte. This improvement not only enhances consumer confidence but also opens doors for wider applications, including aviation and large-scale energy storage. Despite their potential, challenges remain. Solid-state batteries are currently more expensive to produce than lithium-ion batteries, primarily due to the complexity of manufacturing solid electrolytes.
